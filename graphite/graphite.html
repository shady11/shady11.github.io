What is graphite?

  • What is graphite?
  • Graphite pricing

Graphite is a polymer formed purely from carbon in a layered crystal structure, within each layer, the carbon atoms appear in lattice arrangement. This configuration gives graphite several properties that make it a valuable and increasingly widely used mineral.

  • Excellent conductor of heat and electricity
  • Self-lubricating properties within the mineral
  • Natural strength and rigidity
  • Ability to maintain these features at very high temperatures
  • Ability to form compounds with other materials

With its pure carbon and lattice, layered structure graphite resembles diamond, however the atomic structure differs slightly resulting in entirely different properties in both: diamonds being stronger and harder while graphite holds better conductivity and lubricating ability.
